Acids and Bases Formulas

1. pH Formula

Formula: \( \text{pH} = -\log[H^+] \)

Where:

  • pH: Measure of the acidity or basicity of a solution.
  • [H+]: Concentration of hydrogen ions in the solution (in moles per liter).

2. pOH Formula

Formula: \( \text{pOH} = -\log[OH^-] \)

Where:

  • pOH: Measure of the basicity or acidity of a solution.
  • [OH-]: Concentration of hydroxide ions in the solution (in moles per liter).

Relationship between pH and pOH:

\( \text{pH} + \text{pOH} = 14 \)

3. Ion Product of Water (Kw)

Formula: \( K_w = [H^+][OH^-] \)

At 25°C: \( K_w = 1.0 \times 10^{-14} \)

Relationship with pH and pOH:

\( \text{pH} + \text{pOH} = 14 \)

4. Acid Dissociation Constant (Ka)

Formula: \( K_a = \frac{[H^+][A^-]}{[HA]} \)

Where:

  • Ka: Acid dissociation constant.
  • [H+]: Concentration of hydrogen ions.
  • [A-]: Concentration of the conjugate base.
  • [HA]: Concentration of the undissociated acid.

Interpretation: Higher Ka values indicate stronger acids.

5. Base Dissociation Constant (Kb)

Formula: \( K_b = \frac{[OH^-][BH^+]}{[BOH]} \)

Where:

  • Kb: Base dissociation constant.
  • [OH-]: Concentration of hydroxide ions.
  • [BH+]: Concentration of the conjugate acid.
  • [BOH]: Concentration of the undissociated base.

Interpretation: Higher Kb values indicate stronger bases.

6. Relationship between Ka and Kb

Formula: \( K_a \times K_b = K_w \)

Where:

  • Ka: Acid dissociation constant.
  • Kb: Base dissociation constant.
  • Kw: Ion product of water (1.0 × 10-14 at 25°C).

Implication: Knowing either Ka or Kb allows you to calculate the other.

7. Percent Ionization

Formula: \( \text{Percent Ionization} = \left( \frac{[\text{Ionized Acid}]}{[\text{Total Acid}]} \right) \times 100\% \)

Example for an Acid: \( \text{Percent Ionization} = \left( \frac{[H^+]}{[HA]} \right) \times 100\% \)

Interpretation: Indicates the extent to which an acid or base dissociates in solution.

8. Henderson-Hasselbalch Equation

Formula: \( \text{pH} = \text{pK}_a + \log \left( \frac{[\text{A}^-]}{[\text{HA}]} \right) \)

Where:

  • pH: Measure of the acidity of the solution.
  • pKa: Negative logarithm of the acid dissociation constant.
  • [A-]: Concentration of the conjugate base.
  • [HA]: Concentration of the undissociated acid.

Usage: Useful for calculating the pH of buffer solutions.

9. Neutralization Reaction

General Formula:

Acid + Base → Salt + Water

Example: \( \text{HCl} + \text{NaOH} \rightarrow \text{NaCl} + \text{H}_2\text{O} \)

Interpretation: Represents the reaction between an acid and a base to form a salt and water.

10. Strength of Acids and Bases

Strong Acids:

  • Completely dissociate in water.
  • Examples: HCl, HNO3, H2SO4.

Weak Acids:

  • Partially dissociate in water.
  • Examples: CH3COOH, H2CO3.

Strong Bases:

  • Completely dissociate in water.
  • Examples: NaOH, KOH, Ca(OH)2.

Weak Bases:

  • Partially dissociate in water.
  • Examples: NH3, CH3NH2.
