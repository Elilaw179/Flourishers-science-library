Atomic Structure Formulas

1. Bohr's Model of the Hydrogen Atom

Energy of an Electron: En = -13.6 / n2 eV

Radius of Orbit: rn = n2 h2 / 4π2 m e2 Z

2. de Broglie Wavelength

Formula: λ = h / mv

Explanation: Here, λ is the wavelength, h is Planck's constant, m is the mass, and v is the velocity of the particle.

3. Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle

Formula: Δx * Δp ≥ h / 4π

Explanation: This principle states that the uncertainty in position Δx and the uncertainty in momentum Δp are related, and their product is always greater than or equal to h / 4π.

4. Energy of a Photon

Formula: E = hν

Explanation: The energy E of a photon is related to its frequency ν by Planck's constant h.

5. Rydberg Equation

Formula: 1/λ = RH(1/n12 - 1/n22)

Explanation: This equation calculates the wavelength λ of light emitted when an electron transitions between energy levels n1 and n2 in a hydrogen atom, where RH is the Rydberg constant.

6. Atomic Number (Z) and Mass Number (A)

Atomic Number: Z = Number of Protons

Mass Number: A = Number of Protons + Number of Neutrons

7. Relationship between Energy Levels

Formula: ΔE = E2 - E1 = hν

Explanation: The difference in energy ΔE between two energy levels E2 and E1 is equal to the energy of the photon emitted or absorbed, hν.
