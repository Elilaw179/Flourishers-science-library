Elasticity Formulas

1. Hooke's Law

F = kx

Where:

  • F = Force applied (in newtons, N)
  • k = Spring constant (in newtons per meter, N/m)
  • x = Extension or compression (in meters, m)

2. Stress

Stress = F / A

Where:

  • F = Force applied (in newtons, N)
  • A = Cross-sectional area (in square meters, m2)

3. Strain

Strain = ΔL / L

Where:

  • ΔL = Change in length (in meters, m)
  • L = Original length (in meters, m)

4. Young's Modulus

E = Stress / Strain

Where:

  • E = Young's modulus (in pascals, Pa)
  • Stress = Force per unit area (in pascals, Pa)
  • Strain = Ratio of change in length to original length (dimensionless)

5. Bulk Modulus

K = - (ΔP / (ΔV / V))

Where:

  • K = Bulk modulus (in pascals, Pa)
  • ΔP = Change in pressure (in pascals, Pa)
  • ΔV = Change in volume (in cubic meters, m3)
  • V = Original volume (in cubic meters, m3)

6. Shear Modulus

G = Shear Stress / Shear Strain

Where:

  • G = Shear modulus (in pascals, Pa)
  • Shear Stress = Force per unit area (in pascals, Pa)
  • Shear Strain = Ratio of the relative displacement to the original length (dimensionless)

7. Elastic Potential Energy

U = 1/2 × k × x2

Where:

  • U = Elastic potential energy (in joules, J)
  • k = Spring constant (in newtons per meter, N/m)
  • x = Extension or compression (in meters, m)
