Electricity Formulas

1. Ohm's Law

V = IR

Where:

  • V = Voltage (in volts, V)
  • I = Current (in amperes, A)
  • R = Resistance (in ohms, Ω)

2. Power in Electrical Circuits

P = VI

Alternate forms:

  • P = I2R
  • P = V2/R

Where:

  • P = Power (in watts, W)
  • V = Voltage (in volts, V)
  • I = Current (in amperes, A)
  • R = Resistance (in ohms, Ω)

3. Electrical Energy

E = Pt

Where:

  • E = Energy (in joules, J)
  • P = Power (in watts, W)
  • t = Time (in seconds, s)

4. Coulomb's Law

F = k × (|q1q2| / r2)

Where:

  • F = Force between charges (in newtons, N)
  • k = Coulomb's constant (8.99 × 109 N m2/C2)
  • q1, q2 = Magnitudes of the charges (in coulombs, C)
  • r = Distance between the charges (in meters, m)

5. Resistance in Series

Rtotal = R1 + R2 + R3 + ...

Where:

  • Rtotal = Total resistance (in ohms, Ω)
  • R1, R2, R3, ... = Individual resistances in series

6. Resistance in Parallel

1/Rtotal = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + 1/R3 + ...

Where:

  • Rtotal = Total resistance (in ohms, Ω)
  • R1, R2, R3, ... = Individual resistances in parallel

7. Capacitance

C = Q/V

Where:

  • C = Capacitance (in farads, F)
  • Q = Charge (in coulombs, C)
  • V = Voltage (in volts, V)

8. Energy Stored in a Capacitor

E = 1/2 × C × V2

Where:

  • E = Energy (in joules, J)
  • C = Capacitance (in farads, F)
  • V = Voltage (in volts, V)
