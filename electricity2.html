Physics Formulas for High School

1. Static Electricity

Coulomb's Law

F = k * (|q1 * q2|) / r2

Where:

  • F = Electrostatic force between charges (N)
  • k = Coulomb's constant (8.99 × 109 N m²/C²)
  • q1 and q2 = Magnitudes of charges (C)
  • r = Distance between the charges (m)

Electric Field Strength

E = F / q = k * |Q| / r2

Where:

  • E = Electric field strength (N/C or V/m)
  • F = Force on the charge (N)
  • q = Test charge (C)
  • Q = Source charge (C)
  • r = Distance from the source charge (m)

Potential Difference

V = W / q

Where:

  • V = Potential difference (V)
  • W = Work done (J)
  • q = Charge (C)

Capacitance

C = q / V

Where:

  • C = Capacitance (F)
  • q = Charge stored (C)
  • V = Potential difference (V)

2. Current Electricity

Ohm's Law

V = I * R

Where:

  • V = Voltage (V)
  • I = Current (A)
  • R = Resistance (Ω)

Power

P = V * I = I2 * R = V2 / R

Where:

  • P = Power (W)
  • V = Voltage (V)
  • I = Current (A)
  • R = Resistance (Ω)

Kirchhoff's Laws

Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL): The sum of currents entering a junction equals the sum of currents leaving the junction.

Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL): The sum of the potential differences (voltage) around any closed loop or circuit is equal to zero.

Resistance in Series and Parallel

Series: Rtotal = R1 + R2 + R3 + ...

Parallel: 1 / Rtotal = 1 / R1 + 1 / R2 + 1 / R3 + ...

3. Simple A.C. Circuit

Impedance in A.C. Circuit

Z = √(R2 + (XL - XC)2)

Where:

  • Z = Impedance (Ω)
  • R = Resistance (Ω)
  • XL = Inductive reactance (Ω)
  • XC = Capacitive reactance (Ω)

Inductive Reactance

XL = 2πfL

Where:

  • XL = Inductive reactance (Ω)
  • f = Frequency (Hz)
  • L = Inductance (H)

Capacitive Reactance

XC = 1 / (2πfC)

Where:

  • XC = Capacitive reactance (Ω)
  • f = Frequency (Hz)
  • C = Capacitance (F)

Power in A.C. Circuit

P = VIcosθ

Where:

  • P = Power (W)
  • V = Voltage (V)
  • I = Current (A)
  • cosθ = Power factor (dimensionless)
