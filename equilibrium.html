Equilibrium of Forces and Bodies in Liquid

1. Principle of Equilibrium

For a body to be in equilibrium, the sum of forces acting on it must be zero.

Formula:

∑F = 0

Where:

  • ∑F is the sum of all forces acting on the body

2. Principle of Moments

For rotational equilibrium, the sum of clockwise moments about a point must equal the sum of counterclockwise moments about that point.

Formula:

∑Mclockwise = ∑Mcounterclockwise

3. Buoyancy and Archimedes' Principle

When a body is partially or wholly immersed in a fluid, it experiences an upward force called the buoyant force, which is equal to the weight of the fluid displaced by the body.

Formula:

Fb = ρVg

Where:

  • Fb is the buoyant force
  • ρ is the density of the fluid
  • V is the volume of the fluid displaced
  • g is the acceleration due to gravity

4. Conditions for Equilibrium in Fluids

For a body to be in equilibrium in a fluid:

  • The weight of the body (W) must equal the buoyant force (Fb).
  • The center of gravity of the body must be aligned with the center of buoyancy.

Formula:

W = Fb

5. Pressure in a Fluid

The pressure at a depth h in a fluid is given by:

Formula:

P = ρgh

Where:

  • P is the pressure
  • ρ is the density of the fluid
  • g is the acceleration due to gravity
  • h is the depth of the fluid

6. Pascal's Law

A change in pressure applied to an enclosed fluid is transmitted undiminished to every point of the fluid and to the walls of its container.

Formula:

P1 = P2

7. Hydrostatic Pressure

The pressure exerted by a fluid at rest due to the force of gravity.

Formula:

P = P0 + ρgh

Where:

  • P is the total pressure at depth h
  • P0 is the atmospheric pressure
  • ρ is the density of the fluid
  • g is the acceleration due to gravity
  • h is the depth of the fluid
