Expansivity of Solids - Formulas

1. Linear Expansivity (α)

The linear expansivity of a solid is given by the formula:

α = ΔL / (L₀ × ΔT)

Where:
α = Linear expansivity (°C-1)
ΔL = Change in length (m)
L₀ = Original length (m)
ΔT = Change in temperature (°C)

2. Area Expansivity (β)

The area expansivity of a solid is given by the formula:

β = ΔA / (A₀ × ΔT)

Where:
β = Area expansivity (°C-1)
ΔA = Change in area (m²)
A₀ = Original area (m²)
ΔT = Change in temperature (°C)

Note: β ≈ 2α

3. Volume Expansivity (γ)

The volume expansivity of a solid is given by the formula:

γ = ΔV / (V₀ × ΔT)

Where:
γ = Volume expansivity (°C-1)
ΔV = Change in volume (m³)
V₀ = Original volume (m³)
ΔT = Change in temperature (°C)

Note: γ ≈ 3α
