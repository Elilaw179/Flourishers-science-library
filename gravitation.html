Gravitational Field Formulas in Physics

1. Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation

The gravitational force between two masses is given by:

Formula:

F = G * (m1 * m2) / r2

Where:

  • F = Gravitational force between the masses (Newtons, N)
  • G = Universal Gravitational Constant (6.674 × 10-11 N m²/kg²)
  • m1 = Mass of the first object (kg)
  • m2 = Mass of the second object (kg)
  • r = Distance between the centers of the two masses (meters, m)

2. Gravitational Field Strength

The gravitational field strength at a point in space is defined as the gravitational force per unit mass at that point.

Formula:

g = F / m

Where:

  • g = Gravitational field strength (N/kg or m/s²)
  • F = Gravitational force (Newtons, N)
  • m = Mass of the object experiencing the force (kg)

Alternatively, using Newton's law of gravitation:

g = G * (M / r2)

Where:

  • M = Mass of the object creating the gravitational field (kg)
  • r = Distance from the center of the mass creating the field (meters, m)

3. Gravitational Potential Energy

The gravitational potential energy of an object in a gravitational field is given by:

Formula:

U = - G * (m1 * m2) / r

Where:

  • U = Gravitational potential energy (Joules, J)
  • G = Universal Gravitational Constant (6.674 × 10-11 N m²/kg²)
  • m1 = Mass of the first object (kg)
  • m2 = Mass of the second object (kg)
  • r = Distance between the centers of the two masses (meters, m)

4. Escape Velocity

The escape velocity is the minimum speed required for an object to escape from the gravitational field of a planet or other body.

Formula:

ve = √(2GM / r)

Where:

  • ve = Escape velocity (m/s)
  • G = Universal Gravitational Constant (6.674 × 10-11 N m²/kg²)
  • M = Mass of the planet or body (kg)
  • r = Radius of the planet or body from the center to the point of escape (meters, m)

5. Gravitational Potential

The gravitational potential at a point in a gravitational field is defined as the work done per unit mass to bring a small test mass from infinity to that point.

Formula:

V = - G * (M / r)

Where:

  • V = Gravitational potential (J/kg)
  • G = Universal Gravitational Constant (6.674 × 10-11 N m²/kg²)
  • M = Mass of the object creating the gravitational field (kg)
  • r = Distance from the mass creating the field (meters, m)
