Formulas in Heat (Physics)

1. Heat Energy and Specific Heat Capacity

  • Heat Energy: \( Q = mc\Delta T \)
    where:
    \( Q \) = heat energy, \( m \) = mass, \( c \) = specific heat capacity, \( \Delta T \) = change in temperature

2. Latent Heat

  • Heat Energy during Phase Change: \( Q = mL \)
    where:
    \( Q \) = heat energy, \( m \) = mass, \( L \) = latent heat (of fusion or vaporization)

3. Thermal Expansion

  • Linear Expansion: \( \Delta L = \alpha L_0 \Delta T \)
    where:
    \( \Delta L \) = change in length, \( \alpha \) = coefficient of linear expansion, \( L_0 \) = original length, \( \Delta T \) = change in temperature
  • Area Expansion: \( \Delta A = 2\alpha A_0 \Delta T \)
    where:
    \( \Delta A \) = change in area, \( \alpha \) = coefficient of linear expansion, \( A_0 \) = original area, \( \Delta T \) = change in temperature
  • Volume Expansion: \( \Delta V = \beta V_0 \Delta T \)
    where:
    \( \Delta V \) = change in volume, \( \beta \) = coefficient of volume expansion, \( V_0 \) = original volume, \( \Delta T \) = change in temperature

4. Heat Transfer

  • Conduction: \( Q = \frac{kA\Delta T}{d}t \)
    where:
    \( Q \) = heat energy transferred, \( k \) = thermal conductivity, \( A \) = area, \( \Delta T \) = temperature difference, \( d \) = thickness of the material, \( t \) = time
  • Convection: \( Q = hA\Delta Tt \)
    where:
    \( Q \) = heat energy transferred, \( h \) = convective heat transfer coefficient, \( A \) = area, \( \Delta T \) = temperature difference, \( t \) = time
  • Radiation: \( Q = \sigma eA(T^4 - T_0^4)t \)
    where:
    \( Q \) = heat energy transferred, \( \sigma \) = Stefan-Boltzmann constant, \( e \) = emissivity, \( A \) = area, \( T \) = absolute temperature of the object, \( T_0 \) = absolute temperature of the surroundings, \( t \) = time

5. Ideal Gas Law (Related to Heat)

  • Ideal Gas Law: \( PV = nRT \)
    where:
    \( P \) = pressure, \( V \) = volume, \( n \) = number of moles, \( R \) = universal gas constant, \( T \) = temperature
