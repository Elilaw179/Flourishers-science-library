Kinetic Chemistry Formulas

1. Average Rate of Reaction

Formula: Rate = -Δ[A] / Δt

Explanation: This formula calculates the average rate of a reaction, where [A] is the concentration of reactant A, and Δt is the change in time.

2. Rate Law

Formula: Rate = k[A]^m[B]^n

Explanation: The rate law expresses the reaction rate as a function of the concentration of reactants. Here, k is the rate constant, [A] and [B] are the concentrations of reactants A and B, and m and n are the reaction orders.

3. Arrhenius Equation

Formula: k = A e^(-Ea/RT)

Explanation: This equation relates the rate constant k to the temperature T, activation energy Ea, and the frequency factor A. R is the gas constant.

4. Half-Life of a First-Order Reaction

Formula: t1/2 = ln(2) / k

Explanation: This formula gives the half-life t1/2 of a reaction that follows first-order kinetics, where k is the rate constant.

5. Half-Life of a Second-Order Reaction

Formula: t1/2 = 1 / (k[A0])

Explanation: This formula calculates the half-life t1/2 for a reaction of second-order kinetics, where [A0] is the initial concentration of the reactant.

6. Integrated Rate Laws

Zero-Order: [A] = [A0] - kt

First-Order: ln([A]) = ln([A0]) - kt

Second-Order: 1 / [A] = 1 / [A0] + kt

Explanation: These formulas relate the concentration of reactant [A] to time t for zero, first, and second-order reactions respectively. [A0] is the initial concentration, and k is the rate constant.
