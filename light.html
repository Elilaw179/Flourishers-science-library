Reflections and Refraction of Light Formulas in Physics

1. Law of Reflection

The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection.

Formula:

θi = θr

Where:

  • θi = Angle of incidence
  • θr = Angle of reflection

2. Snell's Law (Law of Refraction)

Snell's law relates the angle of incidence and the angle of refraction to the refractive indices of the two media.

Formula:

n1sinθ1 = n2sinθ2

Where:

  • n1 = Refractive index of the first medium
  • θ1 = Angle of incidence
  • n2 = Refractive index of the second medium
  • θ2 = Angle of refraction

3. Refractive Index

The refractive index is the ratio of the speed of light in a vacuum to the speed of light in a medium.

Formula:

n = c / v

Where:

  • n = Refractive index
  • c = Speed of light in a vacuum (approximately 3 × 108 m/s)
  • v = Speed of light in the medium

4. Critical Angle

The critical angle is the angle of incidence above which total internal reflection occurs.

Formula:

sinθc = n2 / n1

Where:

  • θc = Critical angle
  • n1 = Refractive index of the first medium (denser medium)
  • n2 = Refractive index of the second medium (less dense medium)

5. Total Internal Reflection

Total internal reflection occurs when the angle of incidence is greater than the critical angle, causing all the light to reflect back into the medium.

Formula:

θi > θc

Where:

  • θi = Angle of incidence
  • θc = Critical angle

6. Lens Formula

The lens formula relates the focal length, the object distance, and the image distance for a lens.

Formula:

1/f = 1/v - 1/u

Where:

  • f = Focal length of the lens
  • v = Image distance
  • u = Object distance

7. Magnification (m)

Magnification is the ratio of the height of the image to the height of the object.

Formula:

m = hi / ho = v / u

Where:

  • m = Magnification
  • hi = Height of the image
  • ho = Height of the object
  • v = Image distance
  • u = Object distance

8. Power of a Lens (P)

The power of a lens is the reciprocal of its focal length, measured in diopters (D).

Formula:

P = 1/f

Where:

  • P = Power of the lens (in diopters)
  • f = Focal length of the lens (in meters)
