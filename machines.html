Machines in Physics

1. Mechanical Advantage (MA)

Mechanical Advantage is the ratio of the output force (load) to the input force (effort) in a machine.

Formula:

MA = Load (L) / Effort (E)

2. Velocity Ratio (VR)

Velocity Ratio is the ratio of the distance moved by the effort to the distance moved by the load in a machine.

Formula:

VR = Distance moved by Effort (de) / Distance moved by Load (dl)

3. Efficiency (η)

Efficiency of a machine is the ratio of the useful work output to the work input, usually expressed as a percentage.

Formula:

η = (Work output / Work input) × 100%

Since work output is equal to load times load distance and work input is equal to effort times effort distance, efficiency can also be expressed as:

η = (MA / VR) × 100%

4. Work Input

Work input is the work done on a machine, calculated as the product of the effort and the distance moved by the effort.

Formula:

Work Input = Effort (E) × Distance moved by Effort (de)

5. Work Output

Work output is the work done by a machine, calculated as the product of the load and the distance moved by the load.

Formula:

Work Output = Load (L) × Distance moved by Load (dl)

6. Law of the Lever

In a lever, the moments about the fulcrum are equal for equilibrium.

Formula:

Effort (E) × Effort Arm (de) = Load (L) × Load Arm (dl)

7. Types of Simple Machines

The most common types of simple machines include:

  • Lever
  • Inclined Plane
  • Wheel and Axle
  • Pulley
  • Screw
  • Wedge

8. Inclined Plane

An inclined plane reduces the effort needed to lift a load by increasing the distance over which the effort is applied.

Formula:

VR = Length of Incline (L) / Height of Incline (h)

9. Pulley System

In a pulley system, the velocity ratio is equal to the number of strands of the rope supporting the load.

Formula:

VR = Number of supporting strands

10. Screw

A screw converts rotational motion into linear motion.

Formula:

VR = Circumference of Screw / Pitch of Screw
