Linear Momentum Formulas in Physics

1. Linear Momentum (p)

Linear momentum is the product of an object's mass and its velocity.

Formula:

p = mv

Where:

  • p = Linear momentum (kg·m/s)
  • m = Mass of the object (kg)
  • v = Velocity of the object (m/s)

2. Newton's Second Law (in terms of momentum)

Newton's second law states that the rate of change of momentum of an object is equal to the net force acting on it.

Formula:

F = dp/dt

Where:

  • F = Net force acting on the object (N)
  • dp/dt = Rate of change of momentum (kg·m/s2)

3. Impulse (J)

Impulse is the change in momentum of an object when a force is applied over a period of time.

Formulas:

J = Δp

J = FΔt

Where:

  • J = Impulse (N·s)
  • Δp = Change in momentum (kg·m/s)
  • F = Force applied (N)
  • Δt = Time interval over which the force is applied (s)

4. Conservation of Momentum

In the absence of external forces, the total linear momentum of a system remains constant.

Formula (for two objects):

m1v1i + m2v2i = m1v1f + m2v2f

Where:

  • m1 = Mass of the first object (kg)
  • v1i = Initial velocity of the first object (m/s)
  • v1f = Final velocity of the first object (m/s)
  • m2 = Mass of the second object (kg)
  • v2i = Initial velocity of the second object (m/s)
  • v2f = Final velocity of the second object (m/s)

5. Elastic Collision

In an elastic collision, both momentum and kinetic energy are conserved.

Formula (for two objects):

m1v1i + m2v2i = m1v1f + m2v2f

Kinetic energy conservation:

½ m1v1i2 + ½ m2v2i2 = ½ m1v1f2 + ½ m2v2f2

6. Inelastic Collision

In an inelastic collision, momentum is conserved, but kinetic energy is not.

Formula (for two objects):

m1v1i + m2v2i = (m1 + m2)vf

Where:

  • vf = Final velocity after collision (m/s)

7. Recoil Velocity

When an object expels a part of itself (like a bullet being fired from a gun), the remaining part moves in the opposite direction. This is based on the conservation of momentum.

Formula:

vrecoil = - (mbullet * vbullet) / mgun

Where:

  • vrecoil = Recoil velocity of the gun (m/s)
  • mbullet = Mass of the bullet (kg)
  • vbullet = Velocity of the bullet (m/s)
  • mgun = Mass of the gun (kg)
