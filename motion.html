Formulas in Motion (Physics)

1. Basic Kinematic Equations

  • Velocity: \( v = \frac{d}{t} \)
    where:
    \( v \) = velocity, \( d \) = distance, \( t \) = time
  • Acceleration: \( a = \frac{\Delta v}{t} \)
    where:
    \( a \) = acceleration, \( \Delta v \) = change in velocity, \( t \) = time
  • Final velocity (with uniform acceleration): \( v = u + at \)
    where:
    \( v \) = final velocity, \( u \) = initial velocity, \( a \) = acceleration, \( t \) = time
  • Displacement (with uniform acceleration): \( s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2 \)
    where:
    \( s \) = displacement, \( u \) = initial velocity, \( a \) = acceleration, \( t \) = time
  • Final velocity squared (with uniform acceleration): \( v^2 = u^2 + 2as \)
    where:
    \( v \) = final velocity, \( u \) = initial velocity, \( a \) = acceleration, \( s \) = displacement
  • Average velocity: \( v_{avg} = \frac{u + v}{2} \)
    where:
    \( v_{avg} \) = average velocity, \( u \) = initial velocity, \( v \) = final velocity

2. Equations of Motion under Gravity (Free Fall)

  • Velocity: \( v = u + gt \)
    where:
    \( v \) = final velocity, \( u \) = initial velocity, \( g \) = acceleration due to gravity, \( t \) = time
  • Displacement: \( s = ut + \frac{1}{2}gt^2 \)
    where:
    \( s \) = displacement, \( u \) = initial velocity, \( g \) = acceleration due to gravity, \( t \) = time
  • Final velocity squared: \( v^2 = u^2 + 2gs \)
    where:
    \( v \) = final velocity, \( u \) = initial velocity, \( g \) = acceleration due to gravity, \( s \) = displacement

3. Projectile Motion

  • Horizontal Range: \( R = \frac{u^2 \sin 2\theta}{g} \)
    where:
    \( R \) = range, \( u \) = initial velocity, \( \theta \) = angle of projection, \( g \) = acceleration due to gravity
  • Time of Flight: \( T = \frac{2u \sin \theta}{g} \)
    where:
    \( T \) = time of flight, \( u \) = initial velocity, \( \theta \) = angle of projection, \( g \) = acceleration due to gravity
  • Maximum Height: \( H = \frac{u^2 \sin^2 \theta}{2g} \)
    where:
    \( H \) = maximum height, \( u \) = initial velocity, \( \theta \) = angle of projection, \( g \) = acceleration due to gravity

4. Circular Motion

  • Centripetal Acceleration: \( a_c = \frac{v^2}{r} \)
    where:
    \( a_c \) = centripetal acceleration, \( v \) = velocity, \( r \) = radius of the circle
  • Centripetal Force: \( F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r} \)
    where:
    \( F_c \) = centripetal force, \( m \) = mass, \( v \) = velocity, \( r \) = radius of the circle
