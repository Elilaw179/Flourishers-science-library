Nuclear Chemistry Formulas

1. Mass-Energy Equivalence

Formula: E = mc2
Where:
E = Energy (Joules)
m = Mass (kg)
c = Speed of light in vacuum (3.00 × 108 m/s)

2. Radioactive Decay Law

Formula: N = N0 e-λt
Where:
N = Number of undecayed nuclei at time t
N0 = Initial number of undecayed nuclei
λ = Decay constant
t = Time

3. Half-Life

Formula: t1/2 = 0.693 / λ
Where:
t1/2 = Half-life
λ = Decay constant

4. Activity of a Radioactive Substance

Formula: A = λN
Where:
A = Activity (decays per second)
λ = Decay constant
N = Number of undecayed nuclei

5. Binding Energy

Formula: BE = [Δm]c2
Where:
BE = Binding Energy (Joules or MeV)
Δm = Mass defect (kg or u)
c = Speed of light in vacuum (3.00 × 108 m/s)

6. Nuclear Fission Energy Released

Formula: Q = [Δm]c2
Where:
Q = Energy released (Joules)
Δm = Mass defect (kg or u)
c = Speed of light in vacuum (3.00 × 108 m/s)

7. Nuclear Fusion Energy Released

Formula: Q = [Δm]c2
Where:
Q = Energy released (Joules)
Δm = Mass defect (kg or u)
c = Speed of light in vacuum (3.00 × 108 m/s)

8. Rate of Radioactive Decay

Formula: R = -dN/dt = λN
Where:
R = Rate of decay (decays per second)
λ = Decay constant
N = Number of undecayed nuclei

9. Exponential Decay Formula

Formula: N(t) = N0e-λt
Where:
N(t) = Number of undecayed nuclei at time t
N0 = Initial number of undecayed nuclei
λ = Decay constant
t = Time
