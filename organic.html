Organic Chemistry Formulas

1. General Formula for Alkanes

Formula: CnH2n+2
Where:
n = Number of carbon atoms

2. General Formula for Alkenes

Formula: CnH2n
Where:
n = Number of carbon atoms

3. General Formula for Alkynes

Formula: CnH2n-2
Where:
n = Number of carbon atoms

4. General Formula for Alcohols

Formula: CnH2n+1OH
Where:
n = Number of carbon atoms

5. General Formula for Carboxylic Acids

Formula: CnH2n+1COOH
Where:
n = Number of carbon atoms

6. General Formula for Amines

Primary Amine: RNH2
Secondary Amine: R2NH
Tertiary Amine: R3N
Where:
R = Alkyl or aryl group

7. Empirical and Molecular Formulas

Empirical Formula: The simplest whole-number ratio of atoms in a compound.
Molecular Formula: The actual number of atoms of each element in a molecule.

9. Markovnikov's Rule

Statement: "In the addition of HX to an alkene, the hydrogen atom (H) attaches to the carbon with more hydrogen atoms already attached, and the halide (X) attaches to the carbon with fewer hydrogen atoms."

10. IUPAC Nomenclature for Alkanes

General Naming: Prefix + Parent + Suffix
Where:
Prefix = Substituents on the main carbon chain
Parent = Number of carbon atoms in the longest chain
Suffix = Type of hydrocarbon (-ane for alkanes)

11. Structural Isomerism

Formula: Isomers have the same molecular formula but different structural formulas.
Example: Butane (C4H10) and isobutane (C4H10)

12. Functional Groups and Their Formulas

Alcohol: -OH
Aldehyde: -CHO
Ketone: -CO-
Carboxylic Acid: -COOH
Ester: -COO-
Amine: -NH2
Amide: -CONH2
