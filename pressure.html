Pressure in Fluids and Gas Laws Formulas in Physics

1. Pressure (P)

Pressure is defined as the force exerted per unit area.

Formula:

P = F/A

Where:

  • P = Pressure (Pa or N/m2)
  • F = Force applied (N)
  • A = Area over which the force is applied (m2)

2. Pressure in Fluids

The pressure at a certain depth in a fluid is given by:

Formula:

P = ρgh

Where:

  • P = Pressure at depth (Pa)
  • ρ = Density of the fluid (kg/m3)
  • g = Acceleration due to gravity (m/s2)
  • h = Height or depth of the fluid column (m)

3. Pascal's Law

Pascal's Law states that pressure applied at any point in a confined fluid is transmitted equally in all directions.

Formula:

P1 = P2

or

F1/A1 = F2/A2

Where:

  • P1 and P2 = Pressure at different points (Pa)
  • F1 and F2 = Forces applied (N)
  • A1 and A2 = Areas at those points (m2)

4. Boyle's Law (Gas Law)

Boyle's Law states that the pressure of a given mass of gas is inversely proportional to its volume, provided the temperature remains constant.

Formula:

P1V1 = P2V2

Where:

  • P1 and P2 = Initial and final pressures (Pa)
  • V1 and V2 = Initial and final volumes (m3)

5. Charles's Law (Gas Law)

Charles's Law states that the volume of a given mass of gas is directly proportional to its absolute temperature, provided the pressure remains constant.

Formula:

V1/T1 = V2/T2

Where:

  • V1 and V2 = Initial and final volumes (m3)
  • T1 and T2 = Initial and final temperatures (K)

6. Gay-Lussac's Law (Gas Law)

Gay-Lussac's Law states that the pressure of a given mass of gas is directly proportional to its absolute temperature, provided the volume remains constant.

Formula:

P1/T1 = P2/T2

Where:

  • P1 and P2 = Initial and final pressures (Pa)
  • T1 and T2 = Initial and final temperatures (K)

7. Combined Gas Law

The Combined Gas Law combines Boyle's, Charles's, and Gay-Lussac's laws into a single relationship.

Formula:

(P1V1)/T1 = (P2V2)/T2

Where:

  • P1 and P2 = Initial and final pressures (Pa)
  • V1 and V2 = Initial and final volumes (m3)
  • T1 and T2 = Initial and final temperatures (K)

8. Ideal Gas Law

The Ideal Gas Law relates the pressure, volume, temperature, and number of moles of a gas.

Formula:

PV = nRT

Where:

  • P = Pressure (Pa)
  • V = Volume (m3)
  • n = Number of moles of the gas (mol)
  • R = Universal gas constant (8.314 J/mol·K)
  • T = Temperature (K)

9. Pressure-Temperature Law (Amontons's Law)

This law states that the pressure of a gas is directly proportional to its temperature, provided its volume remains constant.

Formula:

P1/T1 = P2/T2

Where:

  • P1 and P2 = Initial and final pressures (Pa)
  • T1 and T2 = Initial and final temperatures (K)
