Physics Formulas for High School

1. Energy Quantization

Planck's Quantum Hypothesis

E = hν

Where:

  • E = Energy of a quantum (Joules, J)
  • h = Planck's constant (6.626 × 10-34 J·s)
  • ν (nu) = Frequency of radiation (Hertz, Hz)

Energy Levels in an Atom

En = - (13.6 eV) / n2

Where:

  • En = Energy of the nth level (Electron Volts, eV)
  • n = Principal quantum number (n = 1, 2, 3, ...)

Bohr's Model of the Hydrogen Atom

rn = n2h2 / (4π2me2)

Where:

  • rn = Radius of the nth orbit (meters, m)
  • h = Planck's constant (6.626 × 10-34 J·s)
  • m = Mass of the electron (9.11 × 10-31 kg)
  • e = Charge of the electron (1.6 × 10-19 C)

Energy of Photons

E = hν

Where:

  • E = Energy of the photon (Joules, J)
  • h = Planck's constant (6.626 × 10-34 J·s)
  • ν = Frequency of the photon (Hertz, Hz)

2. Wave-Particle Duality (Wave Paradox)

de Broglie Wavelength

λ = h / p

Where:

  • λ (lambda) = Wavelength associated with a particle (meters, m)
  • h = Planck's constant (6.626 × 10-34 J·s)
  • p = Momentum of the particle (kg·m/s)

Photon Energy-Momentum Relation

E = pc

Where:

  • E = Energy of the photon (Joules, J)
  • p = Momentum of the photon (kg·m/s)
  • c = Speed of light in vacuum (3 × 108 m/s)

Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle

Δx · Δp ≥ h / 4π

Where:

  • Δx = Uncertainty in position (meters, m)
  • Δp = Uncertainty in momentum (kg·m/s)
  • h = Planck's constant (6.626 × 10-34 J·s)

Energy-Time Uncertainty Relation

ΔE · Δt ≥ h / 4π

Where:

  • ΔE = Uncertainty in energy (Joules, J)
  • Δt = Uncertainty in time (seconds, s)
  • h = Planck's constant (6.626 × 10-34 J·s)

Photoelectric Effect

K.E. = hν - φ

Where:

  • K.E. = Kinetic energy of the emitted electron (Joules, J)
  • h = Planck's constant (6.626 × 10-34 J·s)
  • ν = Frequency of incident light (Hertz, Hz)
  • φ = Work function of the material (Joules, J)
