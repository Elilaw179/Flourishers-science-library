Physics Formulas for High School

1. Radioactivity

Decay Law

N(t) = N0 e-λt

Where:

  • N(t) = Number of undecayed nuclei at time t
  • N0 = Initial number of nuclei
  • λ = Decay constant (s-1)
  • t = Time (s)

Activity

A = λN

Where:

  • A = Activity (Bq)
  • λ = Decay constant (s-1)
  • N = Number of undecayed nuclei

Half-Life

T1/2 = ln(2) / λ ≈ 0.693 / λ

Where:

  • T1/2 = Half-life (s)
  • λ = Decay constant (s-1)

Decay Energy

E = (Δm)c2

Where:

  • E = Energy released during decay (J)
  • Δm = Mass defect (kg)
  • c = Speed of light in vacuum (3 × 108 m/s)

Exponential Decay of Activity

A(t) = A0 e-λt

Where:

  • A(t) = Activity at time t (Bq)
  • A0 = Initial activity (Bq)
  • λ = Decay constant (s-1)
  • t = Time (s)

2. Nuclear Reactions

Mass-Energy Equivalence

E = mc2

Where:

  • E = Energy (J)
  • m = Mass (kg)
  • c = Speed of light in vacuum (3 × 108 m/s)

Nuclear Binding Energy

Eb = (Δm)c2

Where:

  • Eb = Binding energy (J)
  • Δm = Mass defect (kg)
  • c = Speed of light in vacuum (3 × 108 m/s)

Q-Value of Nuclear Reactions

Q = (minitial - mfinal)c2

Where:

  • Q = Energy released or absorbed (J)
  • minitial = Mass of initial products (kg)
  • mfinal = Mass of final products (kg)
  • c = Speed of light in vacuum (3 × 108 m/s)

Energy Released in Fission

E = (Δm)c2

Where:

  • E = Energy released during fission (J)
  • Δm = Mass defect (kg)
  • c = Speed of light in vacuum (3 × 108 m/s)

Energy Released in Fusion

E = (Δm)c2

Where:

  • E = Energy released during fusion (J)
  • Δm = Mass defect (kg)
  • c = Speed of light in vacuum (3 × 108 m/s)
