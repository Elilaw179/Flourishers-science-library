Simple Harmonic Motion (S.H.M.) Formulas in Physics

1. Displacement in S.H.M.

Displacement in simple harmonic motion is given by the equation:

Formula:

x(t) = A cos(ωt + φ)

Where:

  • x(t) = Displacement at time t
  • A = Amplitude of the motion
  • ω = Angular frequency (rad/s)
  • t = Time (s)
  • φ = Phase constant (rad)

2. Velocity in S.H.M.

The velocity of a particle executing simple harmonic motion is the derivative of displacement with respect to time.

Formula:

v(t) = -Aω sin(ωt + φ)

3. Acceleration in S.H.M.

The acceleration of a particle in simple harmonic motion is the derivative of velocity with respect to time.

Formula:

a(t) = -Aω2 cos(ωt + φ)

Acceleration can also be related to displacement:

Alternative Formula:

a(t) = -ω2 x(t)

4. Angular Frequency (ω)

The angular frequency is related to the period (T) and the frequency (f) of the motion.

Formulas:

ω = 2πf

ω = 2π / T

5. Period (T) of S.H.M.

The period is the time taken for one complete cycle of the motion.

Formula:

T = 2π / ω

6. Frequency (f) of S.H.M.

Frequency is the number of oscillations per unit time.

Formula:

f = 1 / T

7. Total Energy in S.H.M.

The total energy of a particle in simple harmonic motion is the sum of its kinetic and potential energies.

Formula:

E = ½ mω2A2

Where:

  • E = Total energy
  • m = Mass of the particle

8. Kinetic Energy in S.H.M.

The kinetic energy of a particle in simple harmonic motion is given by:

Formula:

KE = ½ mω2(A2 - x(t)2)

9. Potential Energy in S.H.M.

The potential energy of a particle in simple harmonic motion is given by:

Formula:

PE = ½ mω2x(t)2

10. Maximum Velocity in S.H.M.

The maximum velocity occurs when the displacement is zero (x = 0).

Formula:

vmax = Aω

11. Maximum Acceleration in S.H.M.

The maximum acceleration occurs when the displacement is at its maximum (x = ±A).

Formula:

amax = Aω2

12. Restoring Force in S.H.M.

The restoring force is proportional to the displacement and acts in the opposite direction.

Formula:

F = -kx

Where:

  • F = Restoring force
  • k = Force constant
