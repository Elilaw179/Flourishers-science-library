Stoichiometry Formulas

1. Mole-Mass Relationship

Formula: \( n = \frac{m}{M} \)

Where:

  • n: Number of moles
  • m: Mass of the substance (in grams)
  • M: Molar mass of the substance (in g/mol)

2. Mole-Volume Relationship for Gases (at STP)

Formula: \( V = n \times 22.4 \, \text{L} \)

Where:

  • V: Volume of the gas (in liters)
  • n: Number of moles of the gas
  • 22.4 L: Molar volume of a gas at STP (Standard Temperature and Pressure)

3. Mole-Mole Relationship

Formula: \( \text{Given moles} \times \frac{\text{Moles of desired substance}}{\text{Moles of given substance}} \)

This formula is used to convert moles of one substance in a chemical equation to moles of another substance using the coefficients from the balanced equation.

4. Mass-Mass Relationship

Steps:

  1. Convert mass of given substance to moles using \( n = \frac{m}{M} \).
  2. Use the mole ratio from the balanced equation to find moles of desired substance.
  3. Convert moles of desired substance to mass using \( m = n \times M \).

5. Limiting Reactant

Steps:

  1. Calculate the moles of each reactant.
  2. Use the balanced equation to determine which reactant will run out first (this is the limiting reactant).
  3. Use the limiting reactant to calculate the amount of product formed.

6. Percent Yield

Formula: \( \text{Percent Yield} = \left(\frac{\text{Actual Yield}}{\text{Theoretical Yield}}\right) \times 100 \% \)

Where:

  • Actual Yield: The amount of product actually obtained from the reaction (in grams or moles).
  • Theoretical Yield: The maximum amount of product that could be formed based on stoichiometric calculations (in grams or moles).

7. Molarity

Formula: \( M = \frac{n}{V} \)

Where:

  • M: Molarity of the solution (moles per liter)
  • n: Number of moles of solute
  • V: Volume of the solution (in liters)

8. Dilution

Formula: \( M_1V_1 = M_2V_2 \)

Where:

  • M1: Initial molarity
  • V1: Initial volume
  • M2: Final molarity
  • V2: Final volume
